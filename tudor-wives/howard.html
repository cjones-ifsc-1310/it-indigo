Wife #5

Katherine Howard

Little Catherine Howard was estimated to be somewhere around 16 or 17 when she entered public life as one of the Maids-of-Honor at Queen Anne of Cleves' court. Known to be the one of the prettiest girls at court at the time, along with Henry's displeasure with Anne of Cleves, spelled disaster for the fifth queen of Henry Tudor. When her wiley uncle, the Duke of Norfolk (yes, the very same uncle Norfolk who betrayed Anne Boleyn at the end), found out that the king was interested in his young neice, he contrived everything he could to ensure that they would get thrown together, and often.

Humble Beginnings

Catherine's father may have been one of the almighty Norfolk sons, but he was one of the much younger, poorer siblings and had to make his own way in the world. While he was off doing whatever he did to make money, Catherine was left in the custody of the Norfolk matriarch, the Dowager Duchess of Norfolk. In the duchess's household there was very little supervision of the dozen or so maidens who were also staying with the Duchess. Therefore little Miss Kitty ended up getting into much mischief in her time there until she left for court in 1539.

A Rose Without a Thorn

Henry was immediately smitten with Catherine's youth and vivacity, even though he was still married to Anne of Cleves. And Catherine, not realizing the danger of it, got swept away with the idea of being Queen Consort. Therefore, Henry and Catherine married in July of 1540, and while married, Henry often referred to Kitty as a "Rose Without a Thorn" and "a jewel of womanhood." Yes. That would be the same exact month that Henry and Anne signed their divorce agreement.

The Downfall

While Kitty was happily playing with her new jewels and furs, and paying no attention, her past came back to the front and center, in the form of a Francis Dereham, the duchess's former secretary, with whom Kitty once had an "entanglement" while in the Dowager Duchess's custody. He came back to court to try to blackmail her with the information in exchange for funds. Kitty attempted to pay him off several times, but he began to divulge their secrets to others, anyway. As a matter of fact, she had actually had a pre-contract (promise to marry) with Dereham before he left the estate to make his fortune elsewhere, and Catherine left for court. It was also later discovered that before Dereham, Kitty had another "entanglement" with Henry Mannox, a music teacher in the duchess's household.

Although neither of these facts were executable offenses against the king, the precontract with Dereham could be binding enough to annull Henry and Catherine's marriage And if all of THAT wasn't trouble enough, Kitty began to have an adulturous affair with Henry's favorite courtier of the Privy Chamber, Thomas Culpepper.

When the affair with Culpepper was discovered (although it was never proven that they had a sexual relationship), more allegations came out against Kitty in the form of her former housemates giving testimony, and sealed her fate. Culpepper and Dereham lost their heads as well.
